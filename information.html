What is a Carbon Footprint?

Your carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases you produce in your daily activities, such as driving a car, using electricity, or eating certain foods. It is measured in terms of carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions.

Why is it Important?

Carbon emissions are one of the leading causes of climate change. Reducing your carbon footprint can help reduce the negative impacts on the environment, including rising global temperatures, melting glaciers, and more extreme weather events.

How to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

  • Use energy-efficient appliances and lighting.
  • Choose renewable energy sources like solar or wind.
  • Walk, bike, or use public transportation instead of driving.
  • Eat a plant-based diet to reduce emissions from food production.
  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste.
